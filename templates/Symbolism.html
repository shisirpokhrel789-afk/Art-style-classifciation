Symbolism

Symbolism is an artistic movement that emerged in the late nineteenth century. Rather than depicting reality directly, Symbolist artists used symbols, mythology, dreams, fantasy, and spiritual themes to express emotions and abstract ideas. Their works often have mysterious and imaginative meanings.

Characteristics

  • Dream-like imagery
  • Mythological subjects
  • Hidden meanings
  • Spiritual themes
  • Emotional expression

Famous Examples

  • The Kiss – Gustav Klimt
  • The Scream – Edvard Munch
  • Isle of the Dead – Arnold Böcklin
  • The Cyclops – Odilon Redon
  • The Apparition – Gustave Moreau
