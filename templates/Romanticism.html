Romanticism

Romanticism was an art movement of the late eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries that emphasized emotion, imagination, nature, and individual freedom. Romantic artists often created dramatic landscapes, historical events, and scenes that inspired awe, mystery, and powerful emotions.

Characteristics

  • Emphasis on emotion
  • Dramatic landscapes
  • Nature as inspiration
  • Historical and heroic subjects
  • Dynamic lighting

Famous Examples

  • Wanderer Above the Sea of Fog – Caspar David Friedrich
  • Liberty Leading the People – Eugène Delacroix
  • The Raft of the Medusa – Théodore Géricault
  • The Hay Wain – John Constable
  • The Fighting Temeraire – J. M. W. Turner
