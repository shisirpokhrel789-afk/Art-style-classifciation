Minimalism

Minimalism is an art movement that emerged during the 1960s in the United States. It focuses on simplicity by removing unnecessary details and emphasizing basic geometric forms, clean lines, limited colors, and open space. Minimalist artists believe that "less is more" and encourage viewers to appreciate the artwork through its essential elements rather than decoration.

Characteristics

  • Simple geometric shapes
  • Limited color palette
  • Minimal details
  • Balance and symmetry
  • Focus on space and form

Famous Examples

  • Die Fahne Hoch! – Frank Stella
  • Untitled – Agnes Martin
  • Red Blue Green – Ellsworth Kelly
  • Bridge – Robert Ryman
  • Untitled Sculptures – Donald Judd
