Pop Art

Pop Art is an artistic movement that emerged during the 1950s and 1960s. It draws inspiration from popular culture including advertisements, comic books, celebrities, newspapers, and everyday consumer products. Pop Art is known for its bold colors, striking graphics, and playful imagery.

Characteristics

  • Bright and vibrant colors
  • Comic-book style illustrations
  • Celebrity portraits
  • Consumer culture themes
  • Bold outlines

Famous Examples

  • Campbell's Soup Cans – Andy Warhol
  • Marilyn Diptych – Andy Warhol
  • Whaam! – Roy Lichtenstein
  • Drowning Girl – Roy Lichtenstein
  • Just what is it that makes today's homes so different, so appealing? – Richard Hamilton
